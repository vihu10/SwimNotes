The quick version

  1. Find the file you just downloaded — it's usually in your Downloads folder, or in your browser's downloads bar (top or bottom of the window).
  2. Open the file. Your device's calendar app opens and shows the event, already filled in.
  3. Confirm by tapping Add / OK / Save. Done — you'll get an alert 30 minutes before.

Step-by-step for your device

Tap the one that matches your device 👇

📱 iPhone & iPad
  1. Tap the Downloads icon next to Safari's address bar, or open the Files app → Downloads.
  2. Tap the .ics file. The Calendar app opens with the event.
  3. Tap Add (top-right). If asked, choose which calendar to use.
  4. The 30-minute alert is already set for you.
🤖 Android
  1. Pull down the notification shade and tap the finished download, or open Files / My Files → Downloads.
  2. Tap the .ics file. If asked which app to use, choose Google Calendar (or your calendar app).
  3. Review the event and tap Save.
  4. If tapping the file does nothing, use the Google Calendar on a computer method below.
💻 Mac
  1. Click the file in your browser's downloads, or open Finder → Downloads.
  2. Double-click the .ics file. Calendar opens and asks where to add it.
  3. Pick a calendar and click OK.
🪟 Windows
  1. Open the .ics from your browser's downloads bar, or the Downloads folder.
  2. Double-click it. It opens in your default calendar (Outlook or Windows Calendar).
  3. Click Save & Close (Outlook) or Save.
  4. Opened in the wrong app? Right-click the file → Open with → choose your calendar app.
🌐 Google Calendar (on a computer)

Use this if the file won't open into your calendar automatically.

  1. Go to calendar.google.com and sign in.
  2. Click the ⚙ gear (top-right) → Settings.
  3. In the left menu, click Import & export.
  4. Under Import, click Select file from your computer and choose the downloaded .ics.
  5. Choose which calendar to add it to, then click Import.
📨 Outlook
  1. On the web: Add calendar → Upload from file → choose the .ics → pick a calendar → Import.
  2. Desktop app: double-click the file; Outlook opens it — click Save & Close.

  • 📝 A short description with your talking points (if you picked a meet).

Troubleshooting

It's not working — help
  • I can't find the file. Check your Downloads folder, or your browser's downloads bar. You can also tap Download again at the top.
  • Nothing happened when I opened it. Your device may not have a default calendar app set. Use the Google Calendar on a computer import above.
  • It opened as text/gibberish. That's the raw file — choose to open it with your calendar app instead of a text viewer.
